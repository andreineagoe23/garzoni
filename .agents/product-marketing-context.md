_Last updated: 2026-04-08_

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Garzoni is a gamified personal finance education platform that turns financial mastery into a craft you build, one lesson at a time.

**What it does:** Garzoni guides learners through structured finance courses — from budgeting and saving to investing, credit, and taxes — with an AI-powered tutor, daily streaks, badges, leaderboards, and real-time financial tools. Learning is progressive and habit-forming, not passive.

**Product category:** Personal finance education / financial literacy app (sits on the shelf next to Duolingo-style learning apps and finance content platforms)

**Product type:** SaaS — consumer subscription with freemium entry

**Business model:** Freemium. Starter tier is free (limited daily actions). Plus and Pro tiers unlock unlimited learning, personalized paths, premium tools, and market insights via monthly or yearly Stripe subscription. 7-day free trial on yearly plans.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Most people know they should understand money better — but the content available is either too shallow (generic blogs), too dense (textbooks), or too fragmented (YouTube rabbit holes). There's no structured, engaging path from "I know nothing" to "I actually understand this."

**Why alternatives fall short:**

- Generic finance apps (Monzo, Revolut): track money, don't teach it
- YouTube / blogs: unstructured, no accountability, no progression
- Formal courses (Coursera, Udemy): long, expensive, no engagement mechanics
- Duolingo-style gamification without finance depth: feels like a toy
- Financial advisors: expensive, inaccessible, overkill for foundational knowledge

**What it costs them:**

- Bad financial decisions from ignorance (debt, missed savings, wrong investments)
- Anxiety and shame around money conversations
- Paying for advice they don't understand well enough to act on

**Emotional tension:** "I should know this by now. Why is this so hard to learn and stick with?"

**Indirect:**

- YouTube personal finance creators — unstructured, no accountability
- Financial advisors / coaches — expensive, not scalable
- Reading books — passive, no feedback loop

**How each falls short:**

- Investopedia: answers questions but doesn't build a skill path
- Khan Academy: high quality but no engagement, feels like homework
- YouTube: great for motivation, terrible for systematic learning
- Finance apps: powerful for tracking, blind to education

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**

- Only platform combining structured finance curriculum + gamification + AI tutor + real-time tools
- Daily habit mechanics (streaks, missions, XP) that actually keep users coming back
- AI tutor available 24/7 — answers the "but what does that actually mean for me?" question
- Tools aren't bolted on — they link directly to lessons, reinforcing learning in context
- Personalized path adapts to user's goals, risk comfort, and experience level

**How we do it differently:** Learning is progressive, habit-forming, and practical — not passive or one-shot.

**Why that's better:** Users build real, durable financial understanding instead of watching a video and forgetting it.

**Why users choose Garzoni:**

- The gamification keeps them coming back when nothing else has
- The AI tutor unlocks things no static content can
- It's the only place that connects all the dots: learn → understand → apply with tools

## Objections

| Objection                           | Response                                                                                                                                                                           |

- Someone looking for investment tips or stock picks — Garzoni is education, not recommendations
- Someone wanting a financial advisor substitute — we teach, not advise
- Complete non-starters who won't engage with any structured learning format

## Switching Dynamics

**Push (away from current approach):**

- "I've watched 50 finance videos and I'm still confused"
- "I read the Notion doc / Reddit thread and forgot it by Tuesday"
- "I know I should be investing but I don't know where to start"

**Pull (toward Garzoni):**

- Structured path that tells them exactly what to learn next
- AI tutor that answers their specific question right now
- Streaks and missions that make them feel progress every day

**Habit (keeping them stuck):**

- Already paying for a finance app (Revolut) and conflating tracking with learning
- Convincing themselves they'll "do a proper course when they have time"
- Low bar of YouTube / podcast that requires no commitment

**Anxiety (about switching):**

- "Is this just another app I'll use for two weeks and abandon?"
- "Will the content be too basic / too advanced for me?"
- "I don't want another subscription"

## Customer Language

**How they describe the problem:**

- "I just never learned this stuff"
- "I know I should be saving/investing but I don't know how"
- "I feel embarrassed that I don't understand my own finances"
- "I start reading and then it gets confusing and I give up"

**How they describe the solution:**

- "Finally makes it click"
- "Like having a finance teacher in your pocket"
- "I actually look forward to doing it every day"
- "Doesn't feel like homework"

**Words to use:**

- master, craft, build, learn, progress, path, habit, understand, practical, clear
- "your financial education", "financial skills", "learning streak"
- "at your own pace", "step by step", "real-world tools"

**Words to avoid:**

- wealth, rich, passive income (feels aspirational/clickbait)
- advice, recommend, suggest (regulatory — not financial advice)
- complex, advanced, expert (intimidating to beginners)
- simple (patronising to intermediate learners)

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Learning path | Structured sequence of courses on a topic (e.g. Investing) |
| Streak | Consecutive days of completing at least one learning action |
| XP | Experience points earned through lessons, quizzes, missions |
| Mission | Daily/weekly challenge tied to a learning or savings action |
| Starter / Plus / Pro | Garzoni subscription tiers (free / paid / premium) |
| Personalized path | AI-recommended learning sequence based on user's goals and profile |

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Confident, warm, precise — like a knowledgeable friend who takes your financial education seriously but never makes you feel stupid for not knowing.

**Style:** Direct, conversational, jargon-light. Short sentences. Avoids fluff. Occasionally uses light metaphor (craft, path, mastery) without going full Renaissance-cosplay.

**Personality:**

- Encouraging without being cheerleader-y
- Smart without being academic
- Ambitious for the user without promising overnight results
- Modern with a subtle timelessness — mastery is a long game

**Renaissance nods (subtle):**
The name Garzoni (Florentine apprentice) anchors a quiet metaphor: you start as an apprentice, you build your craft, you reach mastery. This shows up in language like "your path", "build your knowledge", "the craft of managing money" — never as Renaissance costume, always as a sense of earned progression.
