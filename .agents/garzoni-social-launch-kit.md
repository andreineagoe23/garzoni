## 10 Post Angles

Use these as starting points. Mix across channels and adapt tone per platform.

---

### 1. The "why I built this" story

**Hook:** "I kept seeing the same pattern. People who wanted to understand their money. People who'd tried — and stopped."
**Content:** The problem with passive learning + how Garzoni solves it. End with CTA to sign up free.

---

### 2. The name story

**Hook:** "Why did I name a personal finance app after Renaissance Florentine apprentices?"
**Content:** Explain _garzoni_ — the apprentice who learns a craft through structured practice. The metaphor for how Garzoni approaches financial education. Light, interesting, makes people curious about the product.

---

### 3. The AI tutor angle

**Hook:** "Most finance apps don't answer questions. They give you information. There's a difference."
**Content:** Show what a real AI tutor interaction looks like vs. googling. The "exact question" value prop. Screenshot or demo clip.

---

### 4. The streak/habit angle

**Hook:** "What if learning finance felt like brushing your teeth — not a homework assignment?"
**Content:** The science of habit formation + how streaks make daily learning automatic. Show the streak UI. Link to free signup.

---

### 5. The problem with YouTube finance

**Hook:** "You've watched 20 finance videos. You can name-drop ETFs and compound interest. But your savings rate is the same as it was two years ago."
**Content:** Passive consumption vs. active skill-building. Position Garzoni as the structured alternative.

---

### 6. Feature deep-dive: Learning Paths

**Hook:** "Most people don't know where to start with personal finance. They pick a topic at random and hope it connects."
**Content:** Walk through what a Garzoni learning path looks like — topics, progression, quizzes, certificates. Screenshot or demo clip.

---

### 7. Feature deep-dive: Finance Tools

**Hook:** "Learning about investing without a portfolio analyzer is like learning to drive with no car."
**Content:** Introduce the tools suite — specifically portfolio analyzer and goals reality check — and how they're linked directly to lessons. Demo clip.

---

### 8. The "free to start" angle

**Hook:** "I made it free to start because the biggest barrier to learning personal finance isn't money. It's 'I'll do it later.'"
**Content:** Position Starter tier as removing excuses. No credit card. No commitment. Just start. CTA to sign up.

---

### 9. Building-in-public update

**Hook:** "Week 1: [specific metric or milestone]. Here's what I learned."
**Content:** Share a real number (signups, streaks hit, lessons completed, AI prompts sent). Be honest. People root for founders who share the real journey.

---

### 10. Social proof / early user story

**Hook:** "An early user built a 14-day streak on the investing path. Here's what they said."
**Content:** Share a genuine user interaction or early testimonial. Tag/quote if they're happy to be named.

## Visual Rules

**Color palette:** Dark forest green (#1D5330) + warm gold (#E6C87A) + near-black (#0B0F14)

- Gold is for highlights, badges, and calls to attention
- Green is the primary brand color — use for buttons and active states
- Near-black is the background

**Screenshot/demo guidelines:**

- Always use dark mode UI (matches brand)
- Crop tightly to the feature being shown — no full-window clutter
- Annotate with plain labels if needed (no design jargon)
- If showing the AI tutor, use a real question that a genuine learner would ask

**Tone in visuals:**

- Clean, modern, premium-but-accessible
- No stock photos of smiling people with laptops
- No financial charts with upward arrows and dollar signs
- Prefer: actual product UI, data visualizations, simple graphic text cards

**Text-on-image rules:**

- Use brand fonts: Geist Sans (display) or JetBrains Mono (mono accents)
- Dark green or gold text on dark backgrounds
- Keep overlaid text to 10 words or fewer
