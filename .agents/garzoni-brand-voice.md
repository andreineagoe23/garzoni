# Garzoni Brand Voice & Tone Guide

_Last updated: 2026-04-08_

---

## The Core Idea

Garzoni takes its name from the apprentices of Renaissance Florence — young craftspeople who learned their trade through structured practice under a master. The metaphor is quiet but meaningful: **financial mastery is a craft you build, not information you consume.**

This shapes everything about how Garzoni communicates. We speak like a skilled, knowledgeable friend — someone who takes your financial education seriously, gives you the straight answer, and trusts you to keep going.

---

## Four Tone Pillars

### 1. Mastery over hustle

We believe in steady, earned progress — not life-hacks or get-rich promises. Garzoni is for people who want to actually understand money, not just feel good about it for a week.

**In practice:** We never promise overnight results, passive income, or financial freedom. We promise clearer thinking, better decisions, and skills that compound over time.

### 2. The apprentice journey

Every user starts somewhere. The product is designed to meet them at their level and move them forward — from first lesson to genuine competence. That progression is worth acknowledging and celebrating.

**In practice:** We talk about "where you are on your path", "your next step", "building on what you know". Progress is the product.

### 3. Warm but precise

We're not a textbook and we're not a motivational poster. We explain things clearly, skip jargon where it doesn't add value, and define it when it does.

**In practice:** Short sentences. Plain English. When we use a finance term, we earn it. We never make the reader feel behind for not knowing something.

### 4. Gamified but grounded

Streaks, badges, and leaderboards are tools for building real habits — not ends in themselves. We celebrate them, but we always connect them back to real skill development.

**In practice:** We say "your 7-day streak means you've built a daily learning habit" not "you crushed it!". The reward is the growth, not the badge.

## Renaissance Nods — How to Use Them

The Florence connection is a quiet layer of brand identity, not a costume. It shows up as:

**Acceptable uses:**

- "your path to mastery" — feels earned, not costume-y
- "the craft of managing money" — positions finance as a learnable skill
- "build your financial knowledge" — apprentice-to-master progression
- "your learning path" — a Garzoni-specific term that subtly echoes the journey
- Mission copy: "today's challenge" — echoes a craftsperson's daily practice

**Avoid:**

- Direct Renaissance references ("Like the Florentine bankers of old...")
- Medieval/archaic language ("Embark upon thy financial journey")
- Overusing "mastery" — one per piece of copy, maximum
- Forcing the metaphor into places it doesn't fit naturally

**The test:** If you removed the Renaissance nod and the sentence still works brilliantly, the nod was optional. If the sentence _needs_ the nod to work, rewrite it.

## Word List

### Use these

| Word/phrase              | Why                                     |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| master, mastery          | Core brand metaphor — use sparingly     |
| craft                    | Positions finance as a learnable skill  |
| build                    | Progress-oriented, active               |
| path                     | Personalized, directional               |
| practical                | Grounds abstract content in real use    |
| clear                    | Accessibility without being patronising |
| at your own pace         | Reduces anxiety, emphasizes agency      |
| step by step             | Structured, reassuring                  |
| understand               | The actual goal — not just "know"       |
| habit                    | What daily engagement builds            |
| your financial education | Ownership language                      |

### Avoid these

| Word/phrase       | Why to avoid                                            |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| wealth            | Aspirational cliché — feels like clickbait              |
| rich, get rich    | Wrong positioning — not what we promise                 |
| passive income    | Hustle-culture language, not Garzoni                    |
| advice, recommend | Regulatory — we don't give financial advice             |
| easy, simple      | Patronising; also untrue — finance is genuinely complex |
| hack, shortcut    | Undermines the mastery narrative                        |
| crush it, smash   | Off-brand energy                                        |
| complicated, hard | Don't pre-empt the difficulty; trust the user           |
| just              | Minimises things that aren't minimal                    |
| basically         | Hedging language that erodes trust                      |

## Tone by Context

### Landing page / marketing copy

- Lead with the user's aspiration, not the product's features
- Confident, punchy headline, softer explanatory subtext
- CTAs: active verbs, not passive ("Start learning" not "Get started with learning")
- End sections with forward momentum — always point to the next step

### Onboarding / in-app copy

- Warmer, more conversational
- Celebrate milestones but ground them: "7 days. That's a real habit forming."
- Instructions: short imperative. "Pick the topics you want to master first."
- Error states: honest and helpful, never snarky

### AI tutor responses

- Like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about finance
- Never condescending
- Always offer to go deeper: "Want me to walk through an example?"
- Remind users this is education, not advice when relevant

### Push notifications / streak reminders

- Short, warm, slightly playful
- Reference the streak directly: "5 days running. Keep the craft going."
- Never guilt-trip: "You haven't learned in 2 days 😠" is never acceptable

### Error / empty states

- Honest, brief, forward-looking
- "Nothing here yet — your path starts the moment you do."

## Voice in Practice — Side-by-Side Examples

### Hero headline

| Wrong                                 | Right                                         |
| ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| "Learn about personal finance online" | "Master Finance The Smart Way"                |
| "Get rich with financial education!"  | "Build financial skills that last."           |
| "Finance courses for beginners"       | "Your path to financial mastery starts here." |

### Feature description

| Wrong                                          | Right                                                                                |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| "We have an AI chatbot that answers questions" | "A 24/7 AI tutor that answers your exact question, not a generic one."               |
| "Earn badges when you complete things"         | "Every milestone you hit is a real step forward — and the badge proves it."          |
| "Tools for finance stuff"                      | "Real-world tools linked to the lessons — so you learn it and immediately apply it." |

### CTA

| Wrong                     | Right                       |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| "Sign up now!"            | "Start learning free"       |
| "Subscribe to get access" | "Unlock your learning path" |
| "Click here to register"  | "Begin your path"           |

### Streak notification

| Wrong                            | Right                                       |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| "Don't break your streak! 😤"    | "Day 8. The habit is forming — keep going." |
| "You haven't learned in 2 days." | "Ready to pick up where you left off?"      |

## Brand Voice Summary Card

```
GARZONI SPEAKS LIKE:
  A knowledgeable friend who takes your financial education seriously.
  Confident. Warm. Precise. Never preachy.

GARZONI SOUNDS LIKE:
  "Build financial skills that last."
  "Your path to mastery starts here."
  "The craft of managing money — learned one step at a time."

GARZONI NEVER SOUNDS LIKE:
  "Crush your money goals!"
  "Get rich with our easy tips."
  "Sign up now before it's too late!"

THE RENAISSANCE NOD:
  Quiet. Earned. Never a costume.
  It's in the progression metaphor, not the vocabulary.
```
