# Lesson Authoring Standards (Professional Flow)

This rubric is the baseline for the 9-section lesson flow. **Core principle: concept clarity over content density.** Ask "Is this clear enough to act on?" not "Is this deep enough?"

---

## The Monevo Coach Principle

Every lesson should answer four things clearly:

1. **What is this?** (definition, in plain language)
2. **Why does it affect my money?** (so what)
3. **How do I use it?** (concrete use)
4. **What mistake should I avoid?** (pitfall + takeaway)

A coach doesn’t overwhelm. A coach isolates one concept and builds confidence. If those four are clear, the lesson is doing its job.

---

## Design Goal

Lessons should feel like:

**"I just understood something important in 6 minutes."**

Not: "I just read a finance article."

- **Target reading time per lesson:** ~6 minutes total (mobile-friendly).
- **Depth comes from accumulation of lessons**, not from density per section.
- Gamified apps win on **completion rate**; keep sections tight and scannable.

---

## Mobile Reality

- **45–75 seconds reading per section max** on mobile.
- Scannable paragraphs, clear spacing, no dense walls of text.
- If a lesson takes 12+ minutes to read, users will not complete streaks.

---

## Section-by-Section Philosophy (9 sections)

| #   | Section                   | Purpose                        | Guidance                                                                                      |
| --- | ------------------------- | ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Overview**              | Explain the concept simply.    | One clear definition. Keep it clean. No fluff.                                                |
| 2   | **Core Concept**          | Mechanics + why it matters.    | How it works and why it affects their money. No fluff.                                        |
| 3   | **Knowledge Check 1**     | Test definition and mechanism. | Validates sections 1 & 2.                                                                     |
| 4   | **Applied Insight**       | Show a realistic example.      | e.g. "If you invest £1,000 at 7% annually…" Scenario-led.                                     |
| 5   | **Practical Walkthrough** | Explain what people do wrong.  | e.g. "The biggest mistake is withdrawing gains early…" Pitfall + how to avoid.                |
| 6   | **Knowledge Check 2**     | Test application.              | Validates sections 4 & 5.                                                                     |
| 7   | **Key Takeaways**         | Sharp reinforcement.           | 3–5 short, bullet-style paragraphs. Not motivational quotes. No new heavy theory.             |
| 8   | **Next Steps**            | What to actually do.           | One concrete action. e.g. "Open an investment calculator and test 5, 10, and 20-year growth." |
| 9   | **Watch & Learn**         | Video reinforcement.           | Relevant to the lesson; same tone.                                                            |

---

## Micro-Coaching Model (Text Density)

Replace "minimum character counts" with **clarity and role**:

- **Text 1 & 2 (Overview, Core Concept)**
  - 2 short paragraphs each.
  - 2–3 sentences per paragraph.
  - Clear, direct. No fluff.

- **Text 4 & 5 (Applied Insight, Practical Walkthrough)**
  - Slightly deeper.
  - Include **scenario** (Applied Insight) and **pitfall** (Practical Walkthrough).
  - Still scannable; avoid walls of text.

- **Text 7 & 8 (Key Takeaways, Next Steps)**
  - **Shorter**, sharp reinforcement.
  - No new heavy theory.
  - Key Takeaways: 3–5 short bullets or very short paragraphs.
  - Next Steps: one concrete, doable action.

Lessons stay **tight and powerful**. Depth comes from the full curriculum, not from packing one lesson.

---

## Text Section Standards

- Use clear, professional language. Avoid hype, filler, and vague motivational phrases.
- Tone: **confident, neutral, evidence-based, clear, calm, direct.**
- **No:** "This will change your life," "Millionaires do this," "Crush your goals," "Hack your finances."
- Prefer short paragraphs over dense blocks. No "Action:", "Metric:", "Question:" labels in content.
- Each text section should support the Monevo Coach Principle (what / why / how / mistake) where relevant; not every section must cover all four.

---

## Exercise Standards

- Exercise #1: validate **definition and mechanism** (sections 1 & 2).
- Exercise #2: validate **application** (sections 4 & 5).
- Multiple-choice: one unambiguously correct answer, three plausible distractors, concise wording.
- Avoid generic prompts like "What's one way to use what you learned?"
- Questions at least ~80 characters where possible for context.

---

## Video Standards

- Video must be relevant to the course and lesson topic.
- Use embeddable YouTube URLs: `https://www.youtube.com/embed/<video_id>` or short form from `youtu.be/<video_id>`.
- Place video at section 9 only.
- If a lesson-specific video is missing, fallback to a course-level relevant video.

---

## Structural Standards

- Every lesson has exactly 9 sections in this order:
  `text, text, exercise, text, text, exercise, text, text, video`.
- Section titles: Overview, Core Concept, Knowledge Check 1, Applied Insight, Practical Walkthrough, Knowledge Check 2, Key Takeaways, Next Steps, Watch & Learn.

---

## Cognitive progression (internal only)

For content generation at scale, each text section has an internal role (not shown to users):

| Section | Title                 | Cognitive tag            |
| ------- | --------------------- | ------------------------ |
| 1       | Overview              | Concept Introduction     |
| 2       | Core Concept          | Structural Understanding |
| 4       | Applied Insight       | Risk & Nuance            |
| 5       | Practical Walkthrough | Practical Mechanics      |
| 7       | Key Takeaways         | Strategic Framing        |
| 8       | Next Steps            | Behavioral Reinforcement |

---

## Curriculum Tiers (Strategic)

Tone and complexity should progress across the product:

- **Tier 1 – Financial Foundations**
  Language: very simple (e.g. What is a budget?).

- **Tier 2 – Wealth Mechanics**
  Language: intermediate (e.g. compound interest, diversification).

- **Tier 3 – Market & Strategy**
  Language: analytical (e.g. drawdowns, BRRRR, yield farming, forex leverage).

The tone evolves as users level up so it feels like **progression**, not random topics.
