# Lesson Authoring Standards (Professional Flow)

This rubric is the baseline used to rewrite all lesson sections into the required sequence:

1. Text
2. Exercise (tests text #1)
3. Text
4. Video
5. Text
6. Exercise (tests text #5)
7. Text

## Text Section Standards

- Use clear, professional language. Avoid hype, filler, and vague motivational phrases.
- Each text section should include:
  - concept definition in plain terms,
  - why it matters for financial outcomes,
  - realistic scenario/example,
  - common pitfall and how to avoid it,
  - short practical takeaway.
- Minimum depth target:
  - text #1, #3, #5: at least 700 characters each,
  - text #7: at least 500 characters.
- Prefer short paragraphs over dense blocks.
- Do not use "Action:", "Metric:", "Question:" labels in content.

## Exercise Standards

- Exercise #1 must directly validate understanding of text #1.
- Exercise #2 must directly validate understanding of text #5.
- Use multiple-choice format with:
  - one unambiguously correct answer,
  - three plausible distractors,
  - concise but specific question wording.
- Avoid generic prompts like "What's one way to use what you learned?"
- Questions should be at least 80 characters where possible to maintain context.

## Video Standards

- Video must be relevant to the course and lesson topic.
- Use embeddable YouTube URLs:
  - `https://www.youtube.com/embed/<video_id>`
  - or short form converted from `youtu.be/<video_id>`
- Place video at section 4 only.
- If a lesson-specific video is missing, fallback to a course-level relevant video.

## Structural Standards

- Every lesson must have exactly 7 sections in this exact order:
  `text, exercise, text, video, text, exercise, text`.
- Section titles should be consistent:
  - Overview
  - Knowledge Check 1
  - Core Concept
  - Watch & Learn
  - Applied Insight
  - Knowledge Check 2
  - Key Takeaways
